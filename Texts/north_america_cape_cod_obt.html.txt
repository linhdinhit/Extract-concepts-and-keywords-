 Off the Beaten Track  This island to the south of the Cape is where the well-to-do come to drop their money, not - as the name suggests - where spinster aunts drown their sorrows. Summers at the Vineyard are studded with celebrities, most famously Walter Cronkite - oh, the Clintons are known to drop in, too). But for all the hype, high season is nonetheless a kind of bucolic rush hour, with daytrippers driving off the ferry at the main port of Vineyard Haven and yachties prowling the coast. Oak Bluff is the centre of the holiday merriment, a Victorian-era resort town with porch-wrapped summer houses and a gazebo where bands play, while the Vineyard's other towns still have a foot planted in their whaling origins.
