 Off the Beaten Track  If you thought the Jet d'Eau was big, and the UN was huge, then the scale of CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, will truly impress. Near Meyrin, 10km (6mi) north of Geneva, the centre is not just nerd-vana. The birthplace of the World Wide Web, it also has the world's biggest machine - a 27km (17mi) circular particle accelerator - and the largest magnet. Not surprisingly, it attracts science buffs from the world over.
